Risk Factors

48%

HAVE INFERTILITY

1
Life style

Changes in life style with regards to the food, beverage consumption and long work hours and lack of physical activity add to an already existing difficulty in conception and indirectly to success rates.

2
AGE

Age is a critical factor when trying to start a family. Couples conceiving age on an average is pushed towards 30s instead of 20s earlier and we see more and more people starting families even in the 40s which makes conceiving difficult.

3
STRESS

Stress as we all know is detrimental to hormone imbalance which is crucial for conception and for successful live births. But due to the work and other socio economic factors of current times stress too a crucial factor that is added indirectly.

4
GENETIC

Chromosomal abnormalities, mutations, deletions, translocations, additions and inherited disease will have direct impact on conception.

5
CLINICAL FACTORS

Fertility factors like tubal block, endometriosis, polycystic ovaries, Hernia, varicocele, ED, Azoospermia and non fertility factors such as diabetes, obesity etc contributes their negative impact on success rates.

6
SMOKING

It is known for a while now that smoking adversary when it comes to reproductive health. Many studies have shown that quitting smoking will significantly increases the sperm production and egg quality.
